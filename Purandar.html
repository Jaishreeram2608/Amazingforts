Purandar Overview

1. History:
Purandar is renowned for its historical significance, particularly due to the Purandar Fort, built in the 17th century by the Maratha king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The fort played a crucial role during the Maratha Empire and has several legends associated with it, including the birth of Shivaji's son, Sambhaji.

2. Natural Beauty:
Nestled in the Western Ghats, Purandar is known for its stunning landscapes, lush greenery, and serene environment. The area is dotted with hills, valleys, and waterfalls, making it a popular destination for trekking, hiking, and nature walks.

3. Location:
Purandar is located in Maharashtra, India, approximately 50 kilometers from Pune. Its proximity to Pune makes it easily accessible for weekend getaways, and it is well-connected by road.

Purandar's combination of rich history and natural beauty makes it a notable destination for both history enthusiasts and nature lovers. .
